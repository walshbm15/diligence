# 02 — Stress Test (UK market, researched July 2026)

## Competition
- **Enterprise/legal tier (NOT our market):** Kira, Imprima, Harvey, LegalFly,
  Datasite, Intralinks DealCentre — serve law firms, PE deal teams, and large
  VDR transactions (3,000–10,000 doc data rooms). Their own positioning
  concedes small transactions don't justify enterprise tooling.
- **Adjacent UK startups:** ExitRadar (maps UK exit-ready targets — deal
  SOURCING, upstream of us; potential partner, not competitor).
- **Actual incumbent:** human accountants doing independent business reviews
  at £5k–£25k, and buyers' own accountants doing light-touch reviews. This is
  the price umbrella.
- **Conclusion:** no direct UK competitor found for automated cross-document
  reconciliation sold to sub-£2M buyers/brokers/lenders. Re-verify quarterly.

## Why Diligence OS beat the alternative ideas for UK + 1–4 team
- **Permitry (AI plan review):** US market now crowded — PermitFlow ($54M),
  CivCheck, CodeComply, Blitz AI, Archistar (30+ cities incl. LA County),
  Buildcheck ($5.9M seed, Dec 2025), Helonic, PlanCheckPro, AutoReview.AI.
  UK/EU field still open (post-Grenfell Building Safety Regulator bottleneck
  is real) but requires CV-heavy R&D, corpus grind, and small UK builders are
  hard buyers. Wrong shape for a 1–4 team.
- **Depose (litigation evidence):** built for US common-law discovery;
  UK/EU civil procedure differs; EU AI Act treats justice-administration AI
  as high-risk. US-only play.
- **Recallable (product safety radar):** viable, EU GPSR tailwind, but
  shallowest technically and lowest ceiling.

## Top risks and mitigations
1. **Market ceiling** — UK-only ≈ £1.5–5M ARR realistic capture. Decide
   upfront: bootstrap-profitable company vs VC beachhead story.
2. **Trust deficit year 1** — buyers risking life savings won't trust
   unknown-brand AI alone. Mitigation: ACA-reviewed tier is mandatory at
   launch, not optional.
3. **Garbage-in at SMB level** — shoebox records. Mitigation: document
   sufficiency score is the FIRST output; "insufficient records = red flag"
   is a useful deliverable by design.
4. **Filleted accounts** — thin public data on micro-entities means product
   value depends on seller's private docs. Mitigation: public-data-only scan
   still sellable; external verification (Charges, Gazette, CCJs) works
   WITHOUT seller cooperation.
5. **Accountants turning hostile** — position as their input/white-label
   tool; recruit friendly ACAs before launch.
6. **Liability** — PI insurance quotes month 1; citation-or-refuse enforced
   by critic agent; low-confidence → human review routing.

## POC kill criteria (pre-committed)
- Extraction on SCANNED documents can't exceed ~85% field accuracy even with
  retries → pivot conversation.
- Buyer interviews converge on "I'd only trust my accountant" → pivot to
  selling the engine to accountants as their tool (different but good
  business).
- Fewer than 3 of 5–10 interviewees independently name a price ≥ £300.
