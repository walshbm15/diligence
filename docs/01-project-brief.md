# 01 — Project Brief

## Product
**Diligence OS** — automated cross-document adversarial verification for UK
small-business acquisitions. Target user: individual buyers/searchers doing
£250k–£2M deals (share or asset purchases), plus brokers and acquisition
lenders as distribution channels.

## Problem
- Buyers of small UK businesses can't afford a Quality of Earnings review
  (£5k–£25k from regional accountancy firms) so they buy on trust.
- UK deals are frequently SHARE purchases → buyer inherits ALL historical
  liabilities (tax exposure, employment claims, undisclosed debt).
- UK micro-entities file "filleted" accounts — often no P&L at Companies
  House — so public data alone cannot verify a business. Reconciling the
  seller's private data room against public + tax filings is the only way.

## Market evidence (researched July 2026)
- ~46,000 UK companies are "exit-ready" (established, sole director at/past
  retirement age, no successor) holding ~£158.5B in aggregate assets;
  ~117,000 companies have a sole director in their 70s; 363,557 companies
  have sole directors aged 60+; ~943,000 companies have directors aged 50–60
  aging into the cohort this decade. (ExitRadar UK exit statistics, 2026)
- Ownership at Work estimates more than half of UK owners plan to sell part
  or all of their shareholdings within 10 years.
- Tax forcing function: from April 2026 Business Property Relief /
  Agricultural Property Relief are capped (more business assets in IHT net);
  rising CGT narrows the tax-efficient exit window from both directions.
- Intermediary layer is weak: 1,000+ UK broker firms, unregulated, ~20%
  completion rates (lower for sub-£1M deals).

## Business model
- Per-target report: £400–£1,500. Premium tier: ACA-reviewed report.
- Lead magnet: public-data-only red flag scan + document sufficiency score
  (~£150 or free).
- KEY INSIGHT: ~20% deal completion rates mean a serious buyer evaluates
  3–6 targets before buying one. The unit of sale is "per target evaluated,"
  not "per buyer" — failed deals are still paid work. This substantially
  mitigates the one-shot-customer CAC problem.
- Repeat customers: brokers ("verified listing" reports) and acquisition
  lenders/brokers (Shawbrook, ThinCats, OakNorth ecosystem).
- Market ceiling (honest): UK-only serviceable market ≈ 60–120k reports/yr;
  5% capture at ~£750 avg ≈ £1.5–5M ARR. Great lifestyle/bootstrap business
  for a 1–4 person team; VC-scale only as UK→EU/US beachhead story.

## Distribution
1. UK searcher/ETA community (Searchfunder UK, Twitter/X SMB acquisition scene)
2. Business-transfer marketplaces (BusinessesForSale, Rightbiz) and brokers
3. Accountants as CHANNEL not enemy: report is the input their engagement
   starts from; white-label option. Recruit 2–3 friendly ACAs early.
4. Acquisition finance lenders and their broker networks (year 2)

## Trust & liability posture
- ACA-reviewed premium tier at launch (credibility bridge)
- AI never decides; low-confidence → human verification prompt
- Engagement-letter-grade disclaimers; get PI insurance quotes in month 1
- Cyber Essentials certification; UK data residency; DPAs; retention policy
